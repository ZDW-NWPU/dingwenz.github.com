Short Biography:

I am a Professor at BRAIN Lab, School of Automation, Northwestern Polytechnical University. I lead the Vision & Intelligence Group. I obtained my B.S. and Ph.D. degrees from School of Automation, Northwestern Polytechnical University (NWPU) in 2012 and 2018, respectively. My supervisor is Prof. Junwei Han. From Oct. 2015 to Oct. 2017, I worked with Prof. Fernando de la Torre as a visiting PHD student at the Human Sensing Laboratory in Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).

Research Interest:

I am mainly interested in developing effective computer vision algorithms that are inspired by the human vison and human learning procedure. Currently, I am working on developing weakly supervised learning systems for computer vision tasks like object detection, segmentation, 3D shape reconstruction. I also work on computational visual attention modeling and its application in computer vision tasks like remote sensing imagery analysis and video content analysis.
